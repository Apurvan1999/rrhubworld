Navigating the Do’s and Don’ts of Gifted Reviews

Asmita Published the February 2 2024
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • LinkedIn
Index

In recent times, consumers heavily rely on the recommendations of others when making purchasing decisions, whether it’s choosing a smartphone, selecting cookware, or booking accommodations. Before making a purchase, they prioritize reading reviews to ensure a thorough understanding of both the positives and negatives associated with a product. This is where gifted reviews come handy.

The modern consumer actively seeks opportunities to voice their opinions and values information from authentic and reliable sources. Consequently, the experiences shared by current customers play a pivotal role in influencing potential customers. 

Given the dynamic landscape where brands grapple with evolving expectations, diverse channels, various consumer phases, and the need to establish a distinct brand image, we’ve compiled a set of do’s and don’ts to consider while managing gifted reviews.

Understanding Gifted Reviews:

Gifted reviews involve providing products or services to influencers or customers for free in exchange for an honest review. This strategy aims to:

  1. Build Credibility and Trust
  2. Increase Brand Visibility and Awareness

By leveraging influencers or regular customers, brands can tap into their creativity, garnering diverse and relatable content that speaks directly to potential consumers.

The Do’s of Gifted Reviews

Encourage Genuine Experiences

Product Reviews for T3 Microelectronics

Encouraging genuine experiences is a paramount “do” when managing UGC gifted reviews. It involves fostering an environment where influencers can authentically engage with your product or service. Here’s what you can do:

Instead of providing a rigid script or specific talking points, encourage influencers to explore the product organically and share their authentic experiences. 

  1. Ask reviewers to highlight specific features that stood out to them, providing a well-rounded perspective.
  2. Give them the freedom to express their thoughts in their unique style.

This approach ensures that the content generated is sincere, relatable, and resonates with their audience.

By allowing room for genuine experiences, brands tap into the influencer’s unique voice and storytelling capabilities, creating UGC that feels more like a personal recommendation than a scripted promotion. This not only enhances the credibility of the review but also strengthens the connection between the influencer, the brand, and their audience.

Define Reviews Collection Strategy

From Sprout Social – Social Mentions with Reviews

You should have a pretty good idea of how you want to collect gifted reviews so that you can get visibility on brand engagement and the ship to post rate. You can start by asking influencers to:

  1. Mention you everytime they post a review with your products or services. This will allow their audience with a direct link to your account if they want to further explore the product.
  2. Tag you in their posts in order to let you know about the post. This will also help your and their audience to discover the post and the brand account.

Use a brand hashtag that you give influencers to track the posts. It also helps the audience to explore all the content around your brand. But hashtags can also store spam content that might not be related to your products or to you. So it is highly necessary that you choose a brand term instead of a generic hashtag.

Be Transparent

Transparency stands as a fundamental principle in managing UGC gifted reviews, and it is imperative to clearly disclose when a review is part of a gifted collaboration. This openness is not just a legal requirement but a crucial aspect of maintaining trust with your audience. Influencers, as trusted voices, should explicitly communicate when they have received: 

  1. a product for free or 
  2. as part of a collaboration 

Such transparency is a testament to the brand’s commitment to authenticity and genuine feedback. It assures the audience that 

  1. the influencer’s opinions are not swayed by financial gains, 
  2. and they can rely on the review for an unbiased perspective. 

In an era where consumers prioritize transparency, brands that embrace clear disclosure not only comply with ethical standards but also foster a relationship of trust and credibility with their audience. 

Diversify Your Audience

When gifting products, it’s essential to target a diverse group of users, ensuring a broad spectrum of perspectives and experiences. By doing so, you not only amplify the authenticity of the content but also cater to different demographics within your target audience. Inclusivity in your UGC strategy is paramount, as it helps break down barriers and resonates with a wider range of consumers. 

Your goal should be to create a mosaic of voices that reflects: 

  1. diverse interests, 
  2. preferences, and 
  3. backgrounds of your audience. 

This approach not only strengthens your brand’s connection with various consumer segments but also broadens its appeal. Embracing diversity in your gifted review campaigns ensures that the content produced is relatable to a more extensive audience, fostering a sense of inclusiveness and engagement.

Promote Creativity

Influencers with Creative Content

Fostering creativity among reviewers is a powerful strategy to amplify the impact of your brand. Here’s what you can do:

  1. Encourage reviewers to express their opinions creatively, whether through captivating photos, engaging videos, or eloquent written content. 
  2. Allow for diverse creative expressions to not only enhance the visual appeal of your reviews but also tap into different communication styles that resonate with various audience segments. 

Creative content has a remarkable ability to stand out in the crowded digital landscape, capturing the attention of consumers and leaving a lasting impression. 

Engage with Your Community

Active engagement with your community is a fundamental aspect of a successful strategy for gifted reviews. In addition to responding to your reviewers, you should:

  1. Take the time to craft thoughtful responses 
  2. Express genuine gratitude for the valuable feedback provided by your community 
  3. Engaging in a dialogue with your audience instead of keeping it a one-way communication

It not only strengthens the connection between your brand and your customers, but also demonstrates that you highly value their opinions. 

By acknowledging and appreciating the efforts of those who contribute gifted reviews, you not only build a loyal and supportive community but also encourage others to share their experiences, contributing to the authenticity and richness of your UGC content.

The Don’ts of Gifted Reviews

Avoid Overly Positive Reviews

From Sproutsocial: Constructive Reviews

It’s crucial to steer clear of pressuring users into delivering exclusively positive feedback, as authenticity should always be the guiding principle in your strategy for gifted reviews. You should at all times:

  1. Avoid the temptation to influence or manipulate reviews by discouraging overly positive content. 
  2. Embrace the authenticity that comes with honest reviews, even if they might not be entirely favorable. 

The credibility of your brand is significantly bolstered when customers share their genuine experiences, both positive and negative. By fostering an environment where users feel free to express their authentic opinions, you not only uphold the integrity of your brand but also demonstrate a commitment to transparency and trustworthiness in the eyes of your audience.

Steer Clear of Misleading Information

It is imperative to avoid encouraging reviewers to provide false claims or exaggerate their experiences. Steering clear of misleading information to:

  1. Maintain the integrity of your brand, 
  2. To avoid misrepresentation of your products or services, 
  3. And ultimately harm your brand’s reputation. 

Consumers place a premium on authenticity, and any hint of deception can erode trust. By ensuring that your reviewers are transparent and truthful in their assessments, you not only protect your brand’s credibility but also foster a genuine and trustworthy relationship with your audience.

Don’t Disregard Negative Feedback

Negative Feedback Example

It is crucial not to disregard negative feedback but rather to view it as an opportunity for improvement. Ignoring or dismissing negative reviews can be:

  1. detrimental to your brand’s reputation, 
  2. erode consumer trust, 
  3. and fail to meet consumer expectations.

Embracing constructive criticism allows your brand to demonstrate a commitment to customer satisfaction and continuous improvement. By addressing concerns raised in negative reviews, you not only show your customers that their opinions are valued but also signal a proactive approach to refining your products or services based on genuine user experiences.

Refrain from Excessive Promotions

Refrain from excessive promotions by avoiding the inundation of your audience with an overwhelming number of gifted reviews. While UGC is a powerful tool, flooding your platform with an abundance of reviews may be perceived as insincere and could diminish the authenticity of your brand. 

Striking a balance in your UGC strategy is crucial to: 

  1. maintain credibility and 
  2. prevent your audience from feeling overwhelmed. 

A thoughtfully curated selection of reviews, strategically placed and spaced out, can have a more significant impact, ensuring that each review receives the attention it deserves and contributes to an authentic representation of your brand.

Conclusion

Effectively managing gifted reviews involves striking a balance between authenticity and promotion. By following the do’s and avoiding the don’ts, businesses can create a strategy that not only engages their audience but also builds a foundation of trust and credibility. Remember, UGC is a dynamic and evolving landscape, so adapting your approach based on user feedback and industry trends is key to long-term success.
